The Constitution of India - Adopted on 26 January 1950

üìú About the Constitution

The Constitution of India is the supreme law of the Republic of India. It is the longest written constitution of any sovereign country in the world, with a preamble, 395 articles divided into 8 parts and 12 schedules.

üéØ Preamble

"WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC and to secure to all its citizens:

  • JUSTICE - social, economic and political
  • LIBERTY - of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship
  • EQUALITY - of status and of opportunity
  • FRATERNITY - assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation

Fundamental Rights (Articles 12-35)

  • Right to Equality
  • Right to Freedom
  • Right against Exploitation
  • Right to Freedom of Religion
  • Cultural and Educational Rights
  • Right to Constitutional Remedies

Directive Principles of State Policy (Articles 36-51)

These are guidelines for the government on matters of policy, aiming at securing a social order in which justice (social, economic, and political) is achieved nationally.

Fundamental Duties (Articles 51A)

The fundamental duties of every citizen include respect for the Constitution, safeguarding public property, promoting harmony, developing scientific temper, and striving for excellence.

‚ú® Constitution Day

Constitution Day (or Samvidhan Divas) is celebrated on 26 November every year to commemorate the adoption of the Constitution. On 19 November 2015, the Government of India declared 26 November as Constitution Day.

üîó Amendments

The Constitution can be amended to adapt to changing times and needs of the nation. As of 2024, the Indian Constitution has been amended 105 times, making it a living document that evolves with society.
