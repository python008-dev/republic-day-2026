History of India

Ancient India

India's civilization dates back over 5,000 years, with the Indus Valley Civilization being one of the world's oldest. The Vedic period, Mauryan Empire, and Gupta Empire marked golden ages of science, mathematics, philosophy, and art.

Medieval India

The medieval period saw the rise of various dynasties including the Delhi Sultanate and the Mughal Empire. This era contributed significantly to India's cultural diversity, architecture, and administrative systems.

British Rule

Starting from the 18th century, the British East India Company gradually established control over India. By 1858, India came under direct British Crown rule, leading to economic exploitation and social changes.

Freedom Struggle

The Indian independence movement gained momentum in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Leaders like Mahatma Gandhi, Bhagat Singh, Subhas Chandra Bose, and countless others led the fight for freedom through various methods.

Independence (1947)

After decades of struggle, India gained independence on August 15, 1947. The nation was partitioned into India and Pakistan, marking the end of British colonial rule and the beginning of a new era.

Republic of India (1950)

On January 26, 1950, India adopted its Constitution, becoming a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, the chief architect of the Constitution, ensured it reflected the values of justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity.
